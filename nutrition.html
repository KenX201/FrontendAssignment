Nutrition

Nourish to Flourish: Embracing a Healthy Eating Lifestyle

Nutrition Basics:

These are nutrients your body needs in large amounts to provide energy and build/maintain body structures.

The 3 Main Macronutrients:

Macronutrient Function Source Examples
Carbohydrates Main source of energy Bread, rice, pasta, fruits
Proteins Build & repair tissues, enzymes, hormones Meat, eggs, beans, tofu
Fats Energy storage, hormone production, protect organs Avocados, nuts, oils, butter
  • Calories come from macronutrients.
  • Each gram provides energy:
    • Carbs = 4 kcal
    • Protein = 4 kcal
    • Fat = 9 kcal
Micronutrients are nutrients your body needs in small amounts, but they are essential for proper development, disease prevention, and bodily function.

2 Types of Micronutrients:

Type Function Examples
Vitamins Regulate metabolism, immune function, help in cell repair Vitamin C (citrus), Vitamin A (carrots), Vitamin D (sunlight/fish)
Minerals Bone health, nerve function, fluid balance Calcium, Iron, Magnesium, Potassium
  • Micronutrients do not give energy (no calories), but they help the body use energy from food.
  • Deficiencies can cause health problems like anemia, rickets, or scurvy.

Water

Function of Water Why it is important
Regulates body temperature Through sweat and respiration
Transports nutrients and oxygen Via blood and other fluids
Aids digestion and waste removal Helps with bowel movements and kidney function
Lubricates joints and tissues Keeps your body moving smoothly
  • The human body is ~60% water.
  • You lose water through breathing, sweating, and urination — so regular intake is critical.
