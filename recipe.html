Egg & Veggie Stir-Fry with Brown Rice

Servings:

2 servings

Nutrition Info (Per Serving, Approximate):

  • Calories: ~330
  • Protein: 13 g
  • Carbs: 38 g
  • Fat: 13 g
  • Fiber: 4 - 5 g
  • Sodium: ~500 mg (depends on soy sauce)
Ingredient
Name Picture Comment Amount Required
Eggs Large 2
Cooked Brown Rice (preferably cold/leftover — helps with texture) 1 cup
Mixed Vegies Chopped (e.g., bell pepper, snap peas, carrots, broccoli, zucchini, or a frozen stir-fry mix) 1 cup
Garlic Minced 2 cloves
Fresh Ginger Grated (or 1/4 tsp ground ginger) 1 tsp
Low-Sodium Soy Sauce (or coconut aminos for gluten-free) 1 tbsp
Sesame Oil (or olive oil) 1 tsp
Green Onion Sliced (optional garnish) 1
Chili Flakes (optional for heat) Pinch
Salt (optional, for taste) Appropriate
Pepper (optional, for taste) Appropriate

Instruction

  1. Prep Ingredients:
    • Chop all vegetables and crack eggs into a small bowl (beat lightly).
  2. Cook Veggies:
    • Heat sesame oil in a large nonstick skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    • Add garlic and ginger, sauté 30 seconds.
    • Add veggies and stir-fry for 3–4 minutes, until just tender but still vibrant.
  3. Scramble Eggs:
    • Push veggies to one side of the pan.
    • Pour beaten eggs into the empty side. Let them set slightly, then gently scramble.
    • Once cooked, mix with veggies.
  4. Add Rice & Seasoning:
    • Add the cooked rice to the pan.
    • Drizzle with soy sauce and toss everything together.
    • Stir-fry for another 1–2 minutes to heat through and combine flavors.
  5. Finish & Serve:
    • Taste and adjust salt, pepper, or soy sauce if needed.
    • Garnish with green onions and chili flakes if using.
    • Serve warm!

Notes & Tips:

    Tips:

  • Make it vegan: Swap eggs for tofu or tempeh. Just cube and sauté before the veggies.
  • Spice it up: Add sriracha, chili garlic sauce, or a splash of rice vinegar for zing.
  • More protein? Add an extra egg or some edamame.
  • Rice tip: Use leftover cold rice for better texture. Fresh rice can be too soft.
  • Meal prep: Double the recipe and portion into containers. Stays good in the fridge for 3–4 days.
